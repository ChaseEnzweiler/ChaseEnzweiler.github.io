Trading Strategy

    The goal of this project was to implement the Q-learning model free reinforcement learning algorithm and use it to trade on trade on the stock market. After implementing the algorithm and framing it for stock market trading, the Q-learning strategy was compared to a manually created stock market trading strategy and a specified benchmark strategy. This project was part of Georgia Tech's Machine Learning for Trading course.

In Sample Data

The Q-learning strategy used a combination of 3 different stock trading indicators to form 70 states and could take 3 actions which was to go long, short, or exit position. On in-sample data, both the benchmark strategy (buy and hold) and the manual strategy (buy and sell according to an indicator signal I specified) were outperfomed by the Q-learner.

Out of Sample Data

The Q-learner works by building a "Q" table of states (defined by stock indicators) and best actions (go long, short, or exit) for those states. As the Q-learner is trained the "Q" table updates and converges to an optimal solution. Unfortunatley when testing the Q-learner on out of sample data, the Q-learner performed very poorly compared to the other strategies.
